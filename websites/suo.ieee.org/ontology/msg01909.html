Separately both Matthew West and Graham Horn have presented arguments
against having any formal working documents at this time, even if no
documents are designated as preeminent. Graham has also suggested that
voting for working documents at this time would be too time-consuming and/or
divisive.

So it seems we have a tangle of issues, about which there is some
substantial concern and potential disagreement among multiple voting
members. This tangle of issues needs to be separated and resolved. One way
to resolve these issues might be to have a vote in which the ballot consists
of the following questions, to determine how best to proceed:

1) Should we have any formally designated working documents at all at this
point? (yes, no, abstain)

2) Should any documents be designated as preeminent over others at this
point? (yes, no, abstain)

3) If we have working documents, what procedure should be followed to
designate something as a working document (Choose one):

A) A vote requiring a plurality of voting members in favor of the document?

B) Any voting member may submit a document and it is automatically
recognized as a working document?

C) TBD.

I would like to open the question of whether we should have such a vote for
discussion by all concerned. Maybe this is something that is already clearly
established in the procedures for IEEE working groups? If not, we may need
to establish it for SUO...

> It seems to me that the proposal below, with the amendments
> suggested by John Sowa, has several advantages:
>
> 1) It goes on the record making it clear that SUO is open to
> actively considering multiple "working documents" / "expert
> contributions", etc., at this time. As Chris Partridge noted in
> his recent post, this is something that Jim Schoening had
> previously written, but I think it is good to get this issue
> clearly resolved and established, once and for all.
>
> 2) It also provides a way in which a working document can have
> some degree of formal acknowledgement and status as being under
> consideration by SUO, without having precedence over other
> working documents. This is different from what it seemed that Bob
> Spillers had previously requested, that there be no "formality"
> whatsoever attached to the status of something being a working
> document. Yet some formality without preeminence may be acceptable to all.
>
> The only potential disadvantages and/or issues I can see are the
> following:
>
> A) Does SUO need some kind of "filter" to ensure that not just
> any document can be submitted and automatically accepted as a
> working document / expert contribution, and thereby gain the
> patina of preliminary status with IEEE SUO? For example, suppose
> there is a "crank" who happens to be reading the postings of this
> group, who would like to submit a document that virtually
> everyone else would say is outlandish and not worth considering
> as a working paper? Even under the additional terms proposed
> below, if Mr. Crank waits six months and becomes a voting member,
> then he can submit a document and tell the world it has been
> accepted as a working document by IEEE. One remedy for this
> (hopefully unlikely)potential problem is to require that anything
> submitted for status as a working document must receive at least
> a plurality of votes in favor of acceptance, by voting members of SUO.
>
> B) Separately, Matthew West has raised the question whether
> anything needs to be, or merits being, a formal working document
> at this point. It seems to me that if the Merged Ontology has the
> status of a Working Document, yet the majority of the group
> either abstained or voted against it, then this indicates that
> the Merged Ontology has sufficient limitations that it would be a
> good idea at this point to try to get some other contenders
> recognized as having at least the same, preliminary status, e.g.
> EPISTLE, OpenCyc, etc. In saying this I do not prejudge the
> Merged Ontology's chances of eventually being selected as the
> final SUO standard.
>
> C) We may have multiple working documents, but their relation to
> the final SUO standard is completely open, as it should be at
> this point. The final standard may be one of these working
> documents, chosen as best by the group. Or it may be a "merger"
> of multiple working documents. Or conceivably it might be a
> "metaontology" describing other ontologies. Or it might be some
> framework for translating between ontologies, or some kind of
> "library of ontologies" or "assembly of ontologies", with
> references to member ontologies, etc. Whatever the final standard
> is, it seems that at some point it would have to become a working
> document itself.
