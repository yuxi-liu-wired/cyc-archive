Dear Phil,

> 
> It seems to me that the proposal below, with the amendments 
> suggested by
> John Sowa, has several advantages:
> 
> 1) It goes on the record making it clear that SUO is open to actively
> considering multiple "working documents" / "expert 
> contributions", etc., at
> this time. As Chris Partridge noted in his recent post, this 
> is something
> that Jim Schoening had previously written, but I think it is 
> good to get
> this issue clearly resolved and established, once and for all.
> 
> 2) It also provides a way in which a working document can 
> have some degree
> of formal acknowledgement and status as being under 
> consideration by SUO,
> without having precedence over other working documents. This 
> is different
> from what it seemed that Bob Spillers had previously 
> requested, that there
> be no "formality" whatsoever attached to the status of 
> something being a
> working document. Yet some formality without preeminence may 
> be acceptable
> to all.

MW: If my amendments are accepted, teh formality is that some voting member
(i.e. not just anyone) thinks that this document is worth consideration.
> 
> The only potential disadvantages and/or issues I can see are 
> the following:
> 
> A) Does SUO need some kind of "filter" to ensure that not 
> just any document
> can be submitted and automatically accepted as a working 
> document / expert
> contribution, and thereby gain the patina of preliminary 
> status with IEEE
> SUO? For example, suppose there is a "crank" who happens to 
> be reading the
> postings of this group, who would like to submit a document 
> that virtually
> everyone else would say is outlandish and not worth 
> considering as a working
> paper? Even under the additional terms proposed below, if Mr. 
> Crank waits
> six months and becomes a voting member, then he can submit a 
> document and
> tell the world it has been accepted as a working document by IEEE. One
> remedy for this (hopefully unlikely)potential problem is to 
> require that
> anything submitted for status as a working document must 
> receive at least a
> plurality of votes in favor of acceptance, by voting members of SUO.

MW: I have proposed above what I think is a sufficient condition.
The other control is the peer review that those things submitted
will be subject too. We will need to have an issue log for each document
that is available for all to see. At this stage I think that is enough.
I don't think it is worth not trusting the other members of the list.
The e-mail burden is sufficient to put off any who are not driven to try
to solve the problems we face here.

> 
> B) Separately, Matthew West has raised the question whether 
> anything needs
> to be, or merits being, a formal working document at this 
> point. It seems to
> me that if the Merged Ontology has the status of a Working 
> Document, yet the
> majority of the group either abstained or voted against it, then this
> indicates that the Merged Ontology has sufficient limitations 
> that it would
> be a good idea at this point to try to get some other 
> contenders recognized
> as having at least the same, preliminary status, e.g. 
> EPISTLE, OpenCyc, etc.
> In saying this I do not prejudge the Merged Ontology's 
> chances of eventually
> being selected as the final SUO standard.

MW: EPISTLE and CYC are mentioned as candidate material to be
incorporated into an ontology. I do plan to do some more as soon as
I have time. I would be much more inclined to submit something to
the process Robert I and others have suggested than the current one.
> 
> C) We may have multiple working documents, but their relation 
> to the final
> SUO standard is completely open, as it should be at this 
> point. The final
> standard may be one of these working documents, chosen as 
> best by the group.
> Or it may be a "merger" of multiple working documents. Or 
> conceivably it
> might be a "metaontology" describing other ontologies. Or it 
> might be some
> framework for translating between ontologies, or some kind of 
> "library of
> ontologies" or "assembly of ontologies", with references to member
> ontologies, etc. Whatever the final standard is, it seems 
> that at some point
> it would have to become a working document itself.

MW: If we had a more open process, such as that proposed, I would
expect an evolution. There might be a number of start points, but
with encouragement these might merge at different times until there
were only a few that might be integrated by mapping under some very
high level concepts such as the class that Chris M has introduced in
his proposal (which certainly deserves to be considered as a
Working Document).
