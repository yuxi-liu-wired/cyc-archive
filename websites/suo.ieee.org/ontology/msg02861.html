I do think that concepts have a stable meaning.  Sometimes, the same word 
has a different meaning depending upon the context of its use.  Sometimes, 
the context of use is different but the meaning of an individual word is 
the same.  There are a limited number of different meanings though.  I 
think WordNet has done a good job of describing different word senses.  For 
example, WordNet has several different senses for "eat"

1. eat -- (take in solid food; "She was eating a banana"; "What did you eat 
for dinner last night?")
2. eat -- (eat a meal; take a meal; "We did not eat until 10 P.M. because 
there were so many phone calls"; "I didn't eat yet, fo I gladly accept your 
invitation")
3. feed, eat -- (take in food; used of animals only: "This dog doesn't eat 
certain kinds of meat"; "What do whales eat?")
4. consume, eat up, use up, eat, deplete, exhaust, run through, wipe out -- 
(use up, as of resources or materials; "this car consumes a lot of gas"; 
"We exhausted our savings"; "They run through 20 bottles of wine a week")
5. eat, eat on -- (worry or cause anxiety in a persistent way: "What's 
eating you?")
6. corrode, eat, rust -- (cause to rust; "The acid corroded the metal")

In an ontology we'd need to create a different term (or pattern of terms) 
for each word sense.

Well, I'd formalize these as follows:

(exists (?X ?Y ?Z)
   (and
     (instance-of ?Y MalePerson)
     (instance-of ?X EatingEvent)
     (instance-of ?Z Bread)
     (moistness ?Z Dry)
     (performedBy ?X ?Y)
     (objectDestroyed ?X ?Z)))

(exists (?X ?Y ?Z)
   (and
     (instance-of ?X EatingEvent)
     (instance-of ?Y MalePerson)
     (instance-of ?Z Terrace)
     (performedBy ?X ?Y)
     (occursAt ?X ?Z)
     (illumination ?X Sunny)))

Your last sentence is more of a challenge.  It brings up a number of 
issues.  If I eat in a restaurant am I eating "with" all the other people 
in the restaurant?  Am I eating "with" someone if we don't share the same 
food?  Should the event be decomposed so that there is an EatingEvent which 
the man and his boss participate in as well as individual EatingEvent(s) 
for both the man and his boss that are subparts of the joint event?  As a 
first cut, I'd suggest simply

(exists (?X ?Y ?Z)
   (and
     (instance-of ?X EatingEvent)
     (instance-of ?Y MalePerson)
     (instance-of ?Z Supervisor)
     (manages ?Z ?Y)
     (performedBy ?X ?Y)
     (performedBy ?X ?Z)))

Note that I'm freely making up classes and relations since many of these 
concepts are not yet in the SUMO.
