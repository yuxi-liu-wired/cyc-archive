Could you explain further how the semantics of "on" differs in each of 
these sentences?  It seems the same in each - that there are two physical 
things and one is above and touching another.  The differences I see in the 
sentences are related to the additional information in the sentences.  I 
might decompose #1 above as

"I" take "pink disc"
Purpose of taking "pink disc" is to perform a task involving "pink disc"
I put "pink disc" on "b"
Purpose of putting "pink disc" on "b" is to enable another task
"I" am_capable of putting "green disc" on "c"

In each of these decomposed statements involving "on", that relation has 
the same semantics.

>I have also look a little at 'can'. It is interesting, but I have the same
>problem.

We need a term in the ontology to handle "capability" or the capacity to 
perform an act.
